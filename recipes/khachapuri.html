Khachapuri


This soft, cheese-filled bread is the national dish of Georgia. Part of its charm is that it varies by region.

The open-faced Adjaruli version is filled with cheese and herbs, and garnished with an egg on top.


Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 & 1/2 teaspoons of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1 & 1/2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of instant dry yeast
  • 2 & 3/4 cups of flour

Steps

  1. To make the dough: Heat the butter and milk together in a small saucepan until the butter melts.
  2. Place the sugar, coriander, and salt in a large bowl, and pour the hot milk over it, stirring to dissolve the sugar. Cool to lukewarm.
  3. Weigh your flour; or measure it by gently spooning it into a cup. Stir in the yeast and flour, mixing until a shaggy mass forms. Cover with plastic wrap and let rest for 10 minutes.
  4. After the rest, knead until smooth; 8 to 10 minutes by hand, or 6 to 8 minutes at medium speed in a mixer. Knead in an additional 1 to 2 tablespoons flour if the dough is sticky.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise for 1 to 1 1/2 hours, until it increases in size by at least one third.
  6. To make the filling: Place all the ingredients in a large mixing bowl and beat to combine, or pulse everything together briefly with a food processor; leave some bits of the cheese intact.
  7. To assemble: Line two baking sheets with parchment. Turn the risen dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into four equal pieces.
  8. Roll each piece into an oval shape. Cover with greased plastic wrap, and let rest for 15 minutes.
  9. Spoon one quarter of the cheese mixture into the center of each and spread.
  10. Pull the dough's edges up around the cheese, folding and twisting the ends to form a boat shape.
  11. Cover and let rise for 20 minutes, or until puffy but not doubled. While the breads are rising, preheat the oven to 190°C with two racks toward the center.
  12. Brush the exposed edges of the khachapuri with the egg wash and bake for 15 to 20 minutes, until they feel set.
  13. Remove from the oven and use the back of a spoon to make an indentation in the filling of each khachapuri. Crack an egg into each, place a teaspoon of butter on top, and bake for an additional 8 to 10 minutes (for soft-set eggs), or 12 to 15 minutes (for firmer eggs).
  14. Remove from the oven and serve warm. Garnish with additional fresh herbs.
